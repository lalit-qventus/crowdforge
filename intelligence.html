What "done" looks like
  • ✓ Interviewed 10+ potential users about the problem
  • ✓ Identified a specific, recurring pain point
  • ✓ Found people actively seeking solutions (not just agreeing it's annoying)
  • ✓ Can articulate the problem in one sentence the user would agree with
  • ✓ At least 3 users said they'd pay for a solution
Common mistake: Asking "would you use this?" instead of "tell me about the last time you had this problem."

Playbooks
Linear learning paths
Beginner12 min
Validating Your Idea
How to know if your idea is worth building before writing a line of code.
Start with the Mom Test: never ask "would you use this?" Instead ask about past behavior, current workarounds, and what they've already tried.
Beginner8 min
Finding Co-founders on CrowdForge
The ensemble approach to team formation. Work together first, formalize later.
The best co-founder relationships start with shared work, not shared pitch decks. Post tasks, see who shows up consistently, and let chemistry develop naturally.
Intermediate15 min
Your First 100 Users
Strategies for finding early adopters who genuinely need what you're building.
Go where your users already complain. Reddit threads, Twitter rants, support forums, Hacker News "Ask HN" posts. These are people actively seeking solutions.
Intermediate10 min
Pricing Strategy
How to price your product when you have zero data and maximum uncertainty.
Price based on value, not cost. If your product saves someone $1,000/month, charging $50/month is not expensive. Start higher than you think — you can always lower it.
Advanced20 min
When to Pivot
The metrics, signals, and gut checks that tell you it's time to change direction.
Pivoting isn't failure — it's learning. The signal: you're pushing uphill despite doing everything right. The anti-signal: you haven't tried hard enough yet.

Legal Checklist

Case Studies
Stories from the stage
Intermediate7 min
How TaskFlow Hit $5K MRR
From idea pitch to paying customers in 11 weeks with a 7-person ensemble.
The key insight: they didn't build a better project manager. They built the simplest possible task tracker for freelancers and charged $9/mo from day one.
Advanced10 min
The Pivot That Saved DevKit
Started as a code snippet manager. Became a developer onboarding platform after user interviews revealed the real pain.
Week 6 data showed users weren't saving snippets — they were sharing them with new hires. The team pivoted to onboarding docs in 48 hours. Revenue tripled.
Intermediate6 min
Why PhotoAI Sunset at the Right Time
A case study in knowing when to stop. Strong tech, weak market, and the courage to call it.
D7 retention: 8%. Growth: flat. The ensemble voted to archive after 90 days. Two contributors took learnings to their next project — which hit $10K MRR.
Beginner5 min
NomadDesk: First Revenue in 3 Weeks
A coworking space finder that launched with a single city and a Stripe checkout.
Lesson: launch with one city, one feature, one payment flow. Scale the playbook after proving it works. NomadDesk's first 50 users all came from one Reddit thread.
Advanced12 min
FinTrack's AI Agent Experiment
What happened when 40% of contributions came from AI agents, and how the team balanced human and machine.
AI agents handled boilerplate, tests, and docs. Humans handled architecture, UX, and customer calls. The karma split: 35% AI, 65% human — reflecting validated value.
