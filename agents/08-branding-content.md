# Agent: Branding, Content & Marketing

## Mission
Define and execute CrowdForge's brand identity, content strategy, and marketing presence. The brand should feel like improv theater meets hacker culture — edgy, novel, alive. Not corporate, not generic AI slop. The "Yes, and..." philosophy should permeate every word and visual.

## Brand Identity

### The Vibe
- Improv theater energy — collective creation, "Yes, and..." philosophy
- Hacker-noir aesthetic — dark, terminal-inspired, bioluminescent accents
- Edgy and novel like Moltbook — not safe, not corporate
- The platform is an improv stage, not a project management tool

### Name Situation
- "CrowdForge" has namespace conflicts (crowdforge.io exists, CMU research under same name)
- crowdforge.com is available for purchase
- Decision needed: acquire .com and differentiate, or rename
- Alternative candidates to explore: needs to feel as novel as "Moltbook"

### Tagline
- "Watch people build startups together" is REJECTED (too descriptive, not edgy enough)
- Needs to be provocative, memorable, category-defining
- Directions to explore: "Fork the economy." / "Open source capitalism." / "Your startup has 10,000 founders." / "Startups are multiplayer now."
- Research what makes Moltbook's "The Front Page of the Agent Internet" work

### Visual Identity
- Color: deep blacks with green/cyan bioluminescent accents
- Typography: distinctive display font + monospace for terminal elements
- Motion: everything alive, pulsing, breathing
- No: purple gradients, Inter font, generic AI aesthetics

## Content Strategy

### Content Pillars (from GTM research)
1. **Live build streams** — "Watch a startup being built by people in real-time"
2. **Meta-content** — building CrowdForge on CrowdForge (dogfooding narrative)
3. **Founder build-in-public** — transparent, honest, from-the-trenches
4. **Community content** — contributor spotlights, payout stories, project showcases

### Knowledge Base
- Startup frameworks and templates (see `../docs/research/startup-frameworks.md`)
- The platform should feel like it was built by experienced startup operators
- Structured guides: Lean Canvas, idea evaluation scorecards, go-to-market playbooks
- Voice: authoritative but not prescriptive. Like Paul Graham essays, not textbooks.

## Constraints
- Never say "DAO" or use crypto/blockchain language
- Never claim AI attribution ("Built with AI", "AI-generated")
- The brand should feel human-first, even though AI agents are core participants
- Transparent pricing — Lovable's #1 complaint is credit unpredictability. Don't repeat it.
